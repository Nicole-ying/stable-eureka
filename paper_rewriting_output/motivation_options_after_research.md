# Motivation Options After Research

## Research Summary

- **Scene:** TMLR (ML journal), IMRaD structure, double-blind, 8–15 pages typical, mechanism verification expected over benchmark domination.
- **Exemplar patterns:** Paradigm-contrast opening, component-by-component method, "failure is insight" experiment arc, 3–4 contributions max.
- **SOTA gaps:** (1) No prior LLM reward design system combines structured schema, semantic role attribution, and cross-iteration outcome memory. (2) No prior work integrates explicit risk audit and rollback into LLM reward design.
- **Evidence available:** 10-iteration LunarLander experiment with full trajectory data, attribution traces, memory cards, audit reports. Relaxed-audit variant showing deadlock escape. Multiple ablation configurations (semantic, aligned-balanced).

## Recommended Options (Ranked)

1. **Option A** — Best fit for the available evidence and the "from reward generation to reward search" positioning in the writing plan. Strongest narrative arc. The SOTA gap is clear and mechanism-level. Risk: must not overclaim "iterative" as novel; anchor novelty in memory-augmented iteration.

2. **Option C** — Strong alternative if the user prefers a more diagnostic framing. Attribution becomes the controlling mechanism, with memory and audit as enablers. Fits the experiment data well (attribution traces are rich).

3. **Option B** — Viable but narrower. Best if the user wants to emphasize the safety/audit contribution. The audit deadlock case study is a strong honest-finding narrative.

4. **Option D** — Too narrow for a journal paper unless the attribution method itself is substantially deeper than described. Risk of reviewer pushback on contribution size.
