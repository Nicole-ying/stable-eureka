We will design reward components that are directly computable from the normalized observation vector `obs`, the discrete `action`, and the `done` (terminated) flag. The `info` dictionary may contain additional signals (e.g., leg contacts) but we rely on obs indices 6 and 7 for leg contact. The components are designed to be balanced (each scaled to roughly 0-10 range) and interpretable. We'll implement a reward function that sums these components, with the terminal component dominating at episode end. We'll test the reward by running random rollouts and checking that the agent receives higher cumulative reward for landing softly vs. crashing. The search plan involves iterative tuning of component weights and scaling factors to ensure stable learning.
