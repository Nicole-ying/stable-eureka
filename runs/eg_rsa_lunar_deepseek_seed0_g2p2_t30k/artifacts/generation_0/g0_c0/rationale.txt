The reward function is designed to shape the agent toward soft, upright landings at the pad while minimizing fuel usage. Each component is directly computed from the normalized observation vector and discrete action, avoiding any hidden evaluator details. The velocity and angle penalties encourage slow, stable descent. The distance reward provides a smooth gradient toward the pad. Fuel efficiency penalizes engine firings to reduce waste. Ground contact bonus rewards successful landing posture only when near the pad to avoid false positives. The terminal component gives a large positive reward for a successful landing (both legs down, low speed, upright, near pad) and a large penalty for crashes or out-of-bounds, dominating the total reward at episode end to clearly signal success/failure. All components are scaled to be comparable and within the absolute bound of 1000.
