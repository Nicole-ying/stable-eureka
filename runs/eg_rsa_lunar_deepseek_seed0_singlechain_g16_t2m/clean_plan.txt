## Search Plan for Reward Generation

1. **Initialization**: Start with the proposed reward schema consisting of five components: `shaping`, `velocity_penalty`, `angle_penalty`, `fuel_efficiency`, and `landing_bonus`. The `landing_bonus` is the terminal-outcome component, replacing any crash penalty or terminal shaping.

2. **Exploration Strategy**: Use a genetic algorithm or evolutionary strategy to search over the coefficient space for each component. The coefficients will be sampled from a log-uniform distribution to cover a wide range of magnitudes. The reward function will be evaluated on a fixed set of rollouts (e.g., 10 episodes) to compute a fitness score (e.g., success rate, average return, or a combination).

3. **Evaluation Criteria**: The fitness score will be based on landing success rate, average cumulative reward, and fuel efficiency. Successful landings are defined as: both legs in contact with ground, |x| < 0.1, |y| < 0.1, |vx| < 0.1, |vy| < 0.1, |angle| < 0.1 rad, |angular_velocity| < 0.1 rad/s.

4. **Iteration**: Run the evolutionary search for 100-200 generations, mutating coefficients and recombining top performers. Track the best-performing coefficient set.

5. **Validation**: After finding a promising coefficient set, validate on a larger number of episodes (e.g., 100) to ensure generalization. Tweak coefficients if needed.

6. **Final Reward Function**: The final reward function will be a weighted sum of the five components with optimized coefficients, clipped to [-1000, 1000] to satisfy the bound.
