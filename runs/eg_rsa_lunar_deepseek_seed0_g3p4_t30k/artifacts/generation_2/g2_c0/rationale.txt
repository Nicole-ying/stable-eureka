This candidate builds on g1_c2's successful structure (linear distance penalty, moderate failure penalty, strong progress shaping) while incorporating several targeted improvements based on memory lessons and feedback analysis. Key changes: (1) Increased distance penalty from -0.8*dist to -1.0*dist for stronger gradient, addressing the lesson that linear penalties outperform exponential caps. (2) Added landing_shaping bonus near the pad to provide positive reinforcement for precise approach, potentially preventing the "do nothing" collapse. (3) Increased angle penalty from -0.6 to -0.8 and ground-proximity angle penalty from -0.5 to -0.6 to give stability more influence (reducing the 14:1 progress:stability imbalance). (4) Slightly reduced effort penalties (-0.12 main, -0.04 side) to encourage more active engine use while still penalizing waste. (5) Maintained strong ground-proximity vertical speed penalty (-1.0*abs(vy)) to prevent crashes. The terminal component stays at -15.0 (moderate failure penalty) and +100.0 for success, which worked well in g1_c2. The landing_shaping bonus is designed to be small (max 0.5) so it doesn't dominate but provides a positive gradient toward successful landing conditions. This candidate aims to achieve negative generated_private_gap (conservative shaping) while providing strong, balanced guidance across all components.
