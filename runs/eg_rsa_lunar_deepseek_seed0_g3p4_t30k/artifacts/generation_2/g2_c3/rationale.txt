This candidate builds on g1_c2's successful structure with targeted improvements based on feedback. Key changes: (1) Increased linear distance penalty coefficient from -0.8 to -1.0 to provide stronger gradient (lesson: linear outperforms exponential, and stronger progress shaping helps). (2) Reduced overall speed penalty from -0.5 to -0.4 while keeping the ground-proximity vertical speed penalty, maintaining crash prevention focus. (3) Increased angle penalty from -0.6 to -0.8 to better balance the progress-stability ratio (currently ~14:1, targeting ~10:1). (4) Slightly reduced effort penalties (-0.12 main, -0.04 side) to avoid discouraging engine use while still penalizing waste. The terminal component remains at +100/-15, consistent with the moderate failure penalty lesson. This design aims for negative generated_private_gap (conservative shaping) while providing stronger guidance signals to prevent action collapse.
