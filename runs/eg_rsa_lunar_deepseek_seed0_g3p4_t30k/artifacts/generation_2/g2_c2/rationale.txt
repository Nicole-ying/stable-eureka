This candidate builds on g1_c2's successful approach with targeted improvements based on the feedback analysis. Key changes: (1) Increased linear distance penalty from -0.8 to -1.0 to provide stronger gradient (supported by the lesson that linear outperforms exponential and that progress should be the dominant signal). (2) Increased angle penalty from -0.6 to -0.8 and ground-proximity angle penalty from -0.5 to -0.6 to better balance the progress-stability ratio (currently ~14:1, this moves toward ~10:1). (3) Slightly reduced effort penalties (-0.15→-0.12 main, -0.05→-0.04 side) to encourage active engine use and prevent the "do nothing" action collapse observed in two candidates. (4) Maintained the proven moderate failure penalty (-15) and success reward (+100). The overall structure keeps g1_c2's targeted complexity (ground-proximity speed penalty, proximity angle penalty) which showed the best performance in generation 1.
